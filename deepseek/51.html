question:What is active voice in writing and give examples for using the active voice and inactive voice so that I can see the differences between the two and also see why active is better than inactive.

answer:In writing, voice refers to the relationship between the subject of a sentence and the action described by the verb. There are two main types of voice: active voice and passive voice. **Active Voice:** In active voice, the subject of the sentence performs the action described by the verb. The subject is the doer or the agent of the action. Examples of active voice: - I am writing a letter. (The subject I is performing the action of writing.) - The dog bites the man. (The subject dog is performing the action of biting.) - The manager will review the reports. (The subject manager is performing the action of reviewing.) **Passive Voice:** In passive voice, the subject of the sentence receives the action described by the verb. The subject is the recipient or the object of the action. Examples of passive voice: - A letter is being written by me. (The subject letter is receiving the action of being written.) - The man was bitten by the dog. (The subject man is receiving the action of being bitten.) - The reports will be reviewed by the manager. (The subject reports is receiving the action of being reviewed.) Now, let's compare the two voices: - Active voice tends to be more direct, clear, and concise. It shows who is performing the action and what they are doing. - Passive voice can be less direct and less engaging. It may obscure the doer of the action or make the sentence more wordy. Here's an example that illustrates the difference: Passive: The new policy was announced by the company yesterday. Active: The company announced the new policy yesterday. In the active voice version, the sentence is more direct and clear. We know immediately who performed the action (the company). In the passive voice version, the sentence is less direct, and the doer of the action (the company) is relegated to a secondary role. Using active voice can make your writing more engaging, easier to read, and more effective in conveying your message. However, there are situations where passive voice may be preferable, such as: - When the doer of the action is unknown or irrelevant: The package was delivered yesterday. (We don't care who delivered the package, just that it was delivered.) - When you want to emphasize the action rather than the doer: The play was performed to a packed audience. (The focus is on the performance, not the performers.) In general, though, using active voice can help to create more dynamic, clear, and engaging writing.
