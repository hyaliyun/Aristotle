question:Adam has 50 balls. 20 balls are red, 10 are blue, and the rest are either pink or orange. If there are 3 times as many pink as orange balls, how many orange balls are there?

answer:Adam has a total of 50 balls. Out of the 50 balls, 20 are red and 10 are blue. So the total number of red and blue balls is 20 + 10 = 30. The remaining balls are either pink or orange. Let's assume there are x orange balls. Since there are 3 times as many pink balls as orange balls, there are 3x pink balls. The total number of pink and orange balls is x + 3x = 4x. Therefore, 30 + 4x = 50. Subtracting 30 from both sides, we get 4x = 20. Dividing both sides by 4, we get x = 5. Therefore, there are 5 orange balls. 5 The answer is: 5
